<p><p>The sugar alternative market consists of three main types: High Fructose Syrup (HFS), High Intensity Sweeteners (HIS), and Low Intensity Sweeteners (LIS). HFS is derived from corn and is commonly used in beverages and processed foods due to its cost-effectiveness. HIS includes compounds like aspartame and stevia, offering intense sweetness with minimal calories, ideal for diet products. LIS, such as sorbitol and xylitol, provide moderate sweetness with fewer calories, often used in sugar-free candies and baked goods.</p></p>
