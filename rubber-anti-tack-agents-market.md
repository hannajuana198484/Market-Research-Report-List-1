<p><p>The rubber anti-tack agents market comprises several key types, including stearates, fatty acid esters, fatty acid amides, and soaps. Stearates are salts or esters of stearic acid that provide effective anti-tack properties. Fatty acid esters, derived from fatty acids and alcohols, enhance processability and reduce sticking. Fatty acid amides, with their waxy characteristics, improve lubrication and surface smoothness. Soaps, typically alkaline salts of fatty acids, function as efficient anti-tack agents by reducing adhesion during rubber processing and handling.</p></p>
