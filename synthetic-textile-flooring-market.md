<p><p>The synthetic textile flooring market comprises three primary types: tufting, woven, and needlefelt. Tufting involves stitching yarn into a backing material, creating multi-level textures and patterns suited for residential and commercial use. Woven flooring is created by interlacing fibers on a loom, resulting in durable, high-quality surfaces often used in upscale environments. Needlefelt, produced by entangling fibers with barbed needles, offers a dense, resilient option ideal for heavy traffic areas, providing excellent wear resistance and easy maintenance.</p></p>
