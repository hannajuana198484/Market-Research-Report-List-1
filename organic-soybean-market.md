<p><p>The organic soybean market includes two primary types: fresh organic soybeans and dry organic soybeans. Fresh organic soybeans are typically sold in their pod form, offering a sweet and tender flavor, often preferred for direct consumption or culinary uses. In contrast, dry organic soybeans are harvested, processed, and dried for storage, serving as a staple ingredient in various food products, such as tofu, soy milk, and snacks. Both types cater to health-conscious consumers seeking natural and sustainable options.</p></p>
