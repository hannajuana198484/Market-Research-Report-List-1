<p><p>The shoe orthotics market is primarily divided into two main types: rigid orthotics and soft orthotics. Rigid orthotics are constructed from firm materials, providing strong support and stability for conditions like flat feet or severe pronation. They are typically custom-made to fit the patient's foot precisely. In contrast, soft orthotics are made from flexible materials that cushion and adapt to the foot, offering relief for conditions like plantar fasciitis or general discomfort. Both types aim to enhance foot function and comfort.</p></p>
