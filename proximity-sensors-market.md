<p><p>Proximity sensors are essential in various industries for detecting the presence of objects without physical contact. Inductive sensors use electromagnetic fields for metallic objects, while capacitive sensors detect both metallic and non-metallic items through changes in capacitance. LVDT sensors measure linear displacement with high accuracy. Ultrasonic sensors utilize sound waves to detect distant objects, and photoelectric sensors rely on light beams to sense objects and their position. Each type serves specific applications, optimizing automation and safety in processes.</p></p>
