<p><p>The reactive surfactant market is broadly categorized into anionic and nonionic types, each serving distinct applications. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are known for their excellent emulsifying and wetting properties, making them popular in detergents and industrial applications. Nonionic surfactants, on the other hand, lack any charge and offer superior stability in varying conditions, making them ideal for use in household products and cosmetics. Together, they drive innovation and efficiency across multiple sectors.</p></p>
