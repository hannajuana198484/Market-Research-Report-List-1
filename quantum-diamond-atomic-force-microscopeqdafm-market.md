<p><p>The Quantum Diamond Atomic Force Microscope (QDAFM) utilizes nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers in diamonds for high-resolution imaging and measurements at the nanoscale. It operates in two primary modes: Contact Atomic Force Mode, where the probe maintains a constant contact with the surface to map topography with high precision, and Non-Contact Atomic Force Mode, which measures forces without direct contact, ensuring minimal sample disturbance. Both modes serve various applications in material science, biology, and nanotechnology, catering to diverse research needs.</p></p>
