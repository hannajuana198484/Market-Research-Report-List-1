<p><p>Electrostatic dissipative labels are categorized into two main types based on their application and performance characteristics. Type I labels are designed for environments where static control is critical, primarily used for sensitive electronic components to prevent damage from electrostatic discharge (ESD). Type II labels, while also providing some level of static protection, are generally used in less critical applications, offering moderate ESD control. Both types ensure effective handling and identification of products in ESD-sensitive environments, enhancing safety and performance.</p></p>
