<p><p>Primary Immunodeficiency Diseases (PID) are a group of disorders affecting the immune system, leading to increased susceptibility to infections. Treatment for PID typically involves immunoglobulin replacement therapy, which provides patients with the antibodies they lack. This therapy can be administered intravenously or subcutaneously. Moreover, innovations in immunoglobulin therapies are expanding product offerings available in the market. </p><p>Additionally, the growing aging population and rising prevalence of chronic diseases are contributing factors to market expansion.
